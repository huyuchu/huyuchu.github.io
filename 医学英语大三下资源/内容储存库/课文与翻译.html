Musculoskeletal System

肌肉骨骼系统

1 The musculoskeletal system1, also known as the locomotor system2, gives humans the ability to move using the muscular and skeletal systems. The musculoskeletal system provides form, support, stability, and movement to the body.

肌肉骨骼系统也称为运动系统，它利用肌肉系统和骨骼系统赋予人类运动的能力。肌肉骨骼系统构成身体形状、给身体支撑、维持身体稳定，并让身体可以运动。

CLASSIFICATION OF MUSCLES

肌肉的分类

2 Muscle is often divided into striated muscle3 (or skeletal muscle4), smooth muscle5, and cardiac muscle6. Smooth muscle is under involuntary control and is found in the walls of blood vessels and of structures such as the urinary bladder, the intestines, and the stomach. Cardiac muscle makes up the mass of the heart and is responsible for the rhythmic contractions of that vital pumping organ; it too is under involuntary control.

肌肉通常分为横纹肌（骨骼肌）、平滑肌和心肌。平滑肌不受意识控制，存在于血管壁以及诸如膀胱、肠道和胃等的器官壁中。心肌构成了心脏的主要部分，同时也负责这一至关重要的泵血器官的节律性收缩。心肌不受意识控制。

MUSCLE DISEASES

肌肉疾病

Indications of Muscle Diseases

肌肉疾病的指征

3 Muscular atrophy and weakness are among the most common indications of muscular disease. Pain may be present in muscle disease because of defects in blood circulation, injury, or inflammation of the muscle. Muscle enlargement (muscular hypertrophy) occurs naturally in athletes. Tetany is the occurrence of intermittent spasms, or involuntary contractions, of muscles, particularly in the arms and legs and in the larynx, or voice box. It results from low levels of calcium in the blood and from alkalosis, an increased alkalinity of the blood and tissues. Tetanus, also called lockjaw, is a state of continued muscle spasm, particularly of the jaw muscles, caused by toxins produced by the bacillus.

肌肉疾病最常见的指征包括肌萎缩和肌无力。由于血液循环缺陷、损伤或肌肉炎症，肌肉疾病中也可能出现疼痛。肌肉肥大（肌肉增生）通常发生在运动员身上。抽搐是肌肉的间歇性痉挛或不自主收缩，尤其是手臂、腿部及喉部的肌肉。血液中低钙和碱中毒（血液和组织的碱性增加）都会导致抽搐。破伤风（也叫牙关紧闭症）是由破伤风杆菌所产生的毒素引起肌肉持续性痉挛，尤其是下颌肌肉。

Muscular Dystrophies

肌营养不良

4 The muscular dystrophies are a group of hereditary disorders characterized by progressive muscular atrophy and weakness. In most varieties the muscles of the limb girdles — the pelvic and shoulder muscles — are involved.

肌营养不良是一组遗传性疾病，其特点是进行性肌萎缩和肌无力。大多数情况下，肌营养不良累及肢带肌肉（骨盆和肩膀的肌肉）。

5 Measurement of the activity of creatine kinase (CK)7 in the blood, analysis of a muscle biopsy, and recordings from an electromyograph frequently establish that the muscle weakness is due to primary degeneration of the muscles. Electromyography shows differences in the electrical patterns of normal muscle, myopathy, and chronic denervation, such as in the spinal muscular atrophies.

血液中肌酸激酶（CK）活性的测定、肌肉活组织检查分析和肌电图仪器记录往往可以确定肌无力是否由原发性肌肉变性引起。肌电图检查能显示正常肌肉、肌病及慢性去神经支配时（如脊髓性肌萎缩）电位图形之间的差别。

Myasthenia Gravis

重症肌无力

6 Myasthenia gravis8 is an acquired autoimmune disorder that involves a failure in the transmission of nerve impulses to the muscles and is characterized by persistent muscular weakness and a tendency of muscles to be easily fatigued. Affected individuals have weakness, particularly of the face, limbs, and neck. Symptoms include double vision, difficulty swallowing and breathing, and excessive muscle fatigue during exercise with partial recovery after rest.

重症肌无力是一种获得性自身免疫性疾病，可使神经冲动不能传导给肌肉，其特征是持续性肌无力及肌肉易疲劳。患者会出现肌肉无力，尤其是面部、四肢及颈部，症状包括复视、吞咽困难、呼吸困难，以及运动时肌肉过度疲劳，休息后会部分缓解。

7 Other disorders of muscles include myotonia and myositis as the result of a bacterial or viral infection.

其他的肌肉病变包括肌强直和因细菌或病毒感染引起的肌炎。

SKELETON

骨骼

8 Skeletal system serves as a framework for the body. This framework consists of many individual bones and cartilages. There also are bands of fibrous connective tissue — the ligaments and the tendons — in intimate relationship with the parts of the skeleton.

骨骼系统在人体中起着框架的作用，它由许多单独的骨头和软骨组成。与骨骼紧密相连的还有一些结缔组织带，即韧带和肌腱。

9 The skeleton (Fig. 7.1) is divided into two main parts: axial skeleton9 and appendicular skeleton10. The former includes bones of the skull (Fig. 7.2), the thorax and the vertebral column (spinal column) (Fig. 7.3), and the latter includes those of the shoulders, hips and the extremities.

骨骼（图7.1）分为两大部分：中轴骨骼和附肢骨骼。中轴骨骼包括颅骨（图7.2）、胸骨和脊柱（图7.3），附肢骨骼包括肩部骨骼、髋部骨骼及四肢骨骼。
 

 
 

 
 

 

ARTICULATION

关节

10 Bones articulate with each other or with cartilage, held in place by ligaments and connective tissues to allow various degrees of motion. By degree of mobility, joints are classified as synarthrosis (immovable), amphiarthrosis (slightly movable), and diarthrosis (freely movable).

骨头与骨头相互形成关节或者与软骨形成关节，由韧带和结缔组织固定，这样可以做不同程度的运动。根据活动程度的不同，关节可分为不动关节（固定的）、微动关节（可以轻微移动的）和动关节（能自由活动的）。

11 A typical diarthroses is commonly referred to as synovial joint11 that is held in a capsule lined with synovial membrane and filled with synovial fluid. Synovial fluid provides lubrication for the moving bone surfaces. Diarthroses are further divided into ball-and-socket joints12 (shoulder and hip joints), gliding joints13 (wrists and ankles), pivot joints14 (one between the radius and ulna), hinge joints15 (elbow and knee), and condyloid joints16 (one between the radius and carpals).

典型的动关节通常指滑膜关节。滑膜关节位于关节囊内，其内壁有滑膜覆盖，囊内充满滑液。滑液对运动的骨骼表面有润滑作用。动关节可以进一步分为球窝关节（肩关节和髋关节）、滑动关节（腕关节和踝关节）、车轴关节（桡尺骨关节）、屈戌关节（肘关节和膝关节）和髁状关节（桡腕关节）。

12 Joints allow body part to move in different manners. Movements can be described as abduction, adduction, flexion, rotation, pronation, supination, eversion, inversion, protraction, retraction, and circumduction.

关节能让身体各部分做不同形式的运动。这些运动可以描述为外展、内收、弯曲、旋转、旋前、旋后、外翻、内翻、前伸、回缩及环行运动。

BONE DISEASES

骨骼疾病

13 Changes in bone tissue due to metabolic abnormalities are classified with regard to the amount and composition of the bone tissue. When the amount of bone is lower or higher than normal, the conditions are termed, respectively, osteopenia and osteosclerosis. Generalized osteopenia occurs in osteomalacia, osteoporosis, and osteogenesis imperfecta. Osteosclerosis occurs locally in osteoarthritis, osteonecrosis, and osteomyelitis; it represents an attempt at structural strengthening by thickening of bony trabeculae, but its X-ray appearance may be confused with that of dead bone, retaining its density while adjacent normal bone has become osteopenic.

新陈代谢异常所引起的骨组织变化可以根据骨组织含量和成分进行分类。当骨量低于或者高于正常水平时，就分别称为骨质减少或骨质硬化。骨骼软化、骨质疏松及成骨不全时会发生全身性的骨质减少。骨关节炎、骨骼坏死及骨髓炎会引发局部骨骼硬化，这代表骨骼试图通过骨小梁增厚来加强骨骼结构；但其X线表现可能与死骨混淆，因为死骨的密度不变，而与其相邻的正常骨骼却会出现骨质减少。

14 In women, osteoporosis is caused by a change in the hormonal pattern, and hormone replacement therapy is sometimes used. Weight-bearing and resistance exercises are advocated both as prevention and as therapy in osteoporosis.

女性骨质疏松症是由于激素水平的变化引起的，有时可使用激素替代疗法来治疗。负重运动和抗阻运动被提倡用来预防和治疗骨质疏松症。

BONE FRACTURES

骨折

15 Fracture (Fig. 7.4) is a break in a bone caused by stress. A fracture is called simple (closed)17 when the overlying skin is not broken and the bone is not exposed to the air; it is called compound (open)18 when the bone is exposed. An incomplete, or greenstick, fracture19 occurs when the bone cracks and bends but does not completely break; when the bone does break into separate pieces, the condition is called a complete fracture20. An impacted fracture21 occurs when the broken ends of the bone are jammed together by the force of the injury. A comminuted fracture22 is one in which the broken ends of the bone are shattered into many pieces.

骨折是由于压力引起的骨骼断裂。表面皮肤没有破裂、骨头未暴露在外的骨折称为单纯性骨折（闭合性骨折）；骨头暴露在外的骨折称为复合骨折（开放性骨折）；骨头出现裂纹并弯曲，但不完全断裂的骨折称为不完全骨折或青枝骨折；骨头断裂成分离碎片的骨折称为完全性骨折。当破裂骨骼的末端由于受伤压迫而嵌入另一块骨骼时，这种骨折就称作嵌入性骨折。粉碎性骨折中，骨头的骨折端被粉碎成很多碎片。

16 Fractures can also be classified by their configuration on the bone: a transverse fracture23 is perpendicular to the axis of the bone, while an oblique fracture24 crosses the bone axis at approximately a 45 degree angle. A spiral fracture25, characterized by a helical break, commonly results from a twisting injury.

骨折也可以根据骨折形状来进行分类：横向骨折垂直于骨轴线，斜形骨折与骨轴线横交大约成45°角。螺旋形骨折的特点是螺旋形的裂痕，通常是由于扭转伤所致。
 

 

THERAPEUTIC AND CORRECTIVE MEASURES OF BONE CONDITIONS

骨骼疾病的治疗和矫正方法

17 Traction counteracts muscle pull on the skeleton and is used to reduce and stabilize fractures and to prevent muscle shortening. Osteosynthesis (internal fixation) of bone is aimed at restoration of continuity and stability during healing of a fracture, arthrodesis, or osteotomy. Arthroplasty, aimed at restoration of normal joint motion, is usually performed because of pain and restricted motion — for example, in rheumatoid arthritis of the elbow or the hip — but occasionally to restrict mobility — for example, in recurrent dislocation of the shoulder. Amputation may be performed for several reasons including arterial disease, gross injury, tumors, and developmental abnormality, all more common in the legs than in the arms.

牵引能对抗肌肉在骨骼上的引力，通常用于骨折复位和固定，还用于预防肌肉缩短。接骨术（骨折内固定术）的目的是在骨折、关节固定术和骨切开术的康复期间恢复骨骼的连续性和稳定性。关节成形术旨在恢复正常的关节活动，通常因疼痛和运动受限而进行关节成形术，如类风湿关节炎的肘关节和髋关节；但是偶尔也用来限制活动，如复发性肩关节脱臼。进行截肢手术有多种原因，包括动脉疾病、严重受伤、肿瘤和发育畸形，这些情况在腿部比在手臂更常见。

Nervous System 

神经系统

1 Nervous system①, consisting of the central nervous system② (the brain and spinal cord) and the peripheral nervous system③, conducts stimuli from sensory receptors④ to the brain and spinal cord and impulses back to other parts of the body. 

神经系统由中枢神经系统（脑与脊髓）和周围神经系统构成，将来自感受器的刺激传递到脑和脊髓，并把神经冲动传到身体其他部位。

NERVE CELLS AND NERVES

神经细胞和神经

2 There are two basic cell types within the nervous system: neurons (Fig. 8.1) and neuroglial cells. Each neuron forms dendrites and an axon; axons elongate and form branches, the terminals of which form synaptic connections with a select set of target neurons or muscle fibers. Neuroglial cells or neuroglia can be distinguished from neurons by their lack of axons and by the presence of only one type of process. These cells provide structural support for neurons and play a role in the repair of the central nervous system following injury.

神经系统有两类基本细胞：神经元（图8.1）和神经胶质细胞。每一个神经元都有多个树突和一个轴突；轴突延长产生分支，其末端通过形成突触有选择性地与一组目标神经元或肌纤维相连接。胶质细胞（神经胶质）与神经元的区别在于它们没有轴突，而且仅有一种突起。神经胶质细胞为神经元提供结构上的支持，并在中枢神经系统受伤后的修复过程中起重要作用。
 

 

CENTRAL NERVOUS SYSTEM 

中枢神经系统

3 The central nervous system consists of the brain and spinal cord. Both are surrounded by protective membranes called the meninges, and both float in a crystal-clear cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)⑤. The brain is encased in the neurocranium, while the cylindrical and elongated spinal cord lies in the vertebral canal.

中枢神经系统由脑和脊髓组成，两者都被称为脑脊膜的保护性薄膜包裹，而且都漂浮在清澈透明的脑脊液中。脑位于脑颅内，而细长的圆柱形脊髓则位于椎管内。

4 The brain consists of three major divisions: (1) the cerebrum, (2) the brainstem, consisting of the thalamus, hypothalamus, epithalamus, subthalamus, midbrain, pons, and medulla oblongata, and (3) the cerebellum. 

脑分为3个主要部分：①大脑；②脑干，由丘脑、下丘脑、上丘脑、底丘脑、中脑、脑桥和延髓组成；③小脑。

Cerebrum 

大脑

5 The cerebrum is divided into two hemispheres by the longitudinal fissure. The outer layer of the duplicate cerebral hemispheres is composed of a convoluted outer layer of gray matter⑥, called the cerebral cortex⑦. Beneath the cerebral cortex is an inner core of white matter⑧, which is composed of myelinated commissural nerve fibers connecting the cerebral hemispheres via the corpus callosum⑨, and association fibers connecting different regions of a single hemisphere. 

大脑被纵裂分为左右两个脑半球。两个脑半球相同，其外层是由灰质构成的卷曲双层结构，称为大脑皮质。大脑皮质下面是白质的内核，由两种纤维构成。一种为有髓鞘的连合神经纤维，这些神经纤维通过胼胝体把大脑左右两半球相连；另一种为连结单侧半球不同区域的联合纤维。

6 The cerebral cortex is highly convoluted; the crest of a single convolution is known as a gyrus, and the fissure between two gyri is known as a sulcus. Based on the constant pattern formed by gyri and sulci, the surface of each cerebral hemisphere is commonly divided into four lobes: (1) frontal, (2) parietal, (3) temporal, and (4) occipital. 

大脑皮质高度卷曲；单个卷曲的隆起称为脑回，两个脑回间的裂缝被称为脑沟。根据脑回和脑沟这一恒定的组合模式，每个脑半球的表层通常被分为4叶：①额叶；②顶叶；③颞叶：④枕叶。

Brainstem

脑干

7 The brainstem is made up of all the unpaired structures that connect the cerebrum with the spinal cord. Most rostral in the brainstem are structures often collectively referred to as the diencephalon. These structures are the epithalamus, the thalamus, the hypothalamus, and the subthalamus. Directly beneath the diencephalon is the midbrain, or mesencephalon, and beneath the midbrain are the pons and medulla oblongata, often referred to as the hindbrain. 

脑干由许多不成对的结构组成，这些结构把大脑与脊髓连接。脑干的大部分头端结构通常称为间脑，包括上丘脑、丘脑、下丘脑和底丘脑。间脑的正下方是中脑，而中脑的下方则是脑桥和延髓，通常称为后脑。

Cerebellum 

小脑

8 The cerebellum (“little brain”) overlies the posterior aspect of the pons and medulla oblongata and fills the greater part of the posterior fossa of the skull. 

小脑覆盖在脑桥和延髓后面，占据了后颅窝的大部分空间。

Spinal Cord 

脊髓

9 The spinal cord extends from the medulla oblongata to the terminal part called the conus medullaris. The spinal cord is composed of long tracts of myelinated nerve fibers (known as white matter) arranged around the periphery of a symmetrical butterfly-shaped cellular matrix of gray matter. The gray matter contains cell bodies, unmyelinated motor neuron fibers, and interneurons connecting either the two sides of the cord or the dorsal and ventral ganglia. The gray matter forms three pairs of horns throughout most of the spinal cord: the dorsal horns, the lateral horns, and the ventral horns. 

脊髓从延髓开始延伸到称为脊髓圆锥的终端部分。脊髓由有髓鞘的神经纤维长束（称为白质）构成，排列在一个对称的蝶形细胞基质（灰质）周围。灰质包含细胞体、无髓鞘运动神经纤维和中间神经元。中间神经元有的连接脊髓两侧，有的连接背侧和腹侧的神经节。在整个脊髓的大部分，灰质形成3个对角：后角、侧角和前角。

THE PERIPHERAL NERVOUS SYSTEM

外周神经系统

10 The peripheral nervous system (Fig. 8.2) is composed of cranial nerves, spinal nerves, and certain parts of the autonomic nervous system⑩. There are 31 pairs of spinal nerves: 8 cervical (designated C1-C8), 12 thoracic (T1-T12), 5 lumbar (L1-L5), 5 sacral (S1-S5), and 1 coccygeal (Coc1). Each spinal nerve exits the vertebral canal through an opening called the intervertebral foramen⑪. Lumbar and sacral spinal roots produce a group of nerve roots at the conus medullaris⑫ known as the cauda equina⑬. 

外周神经系统（图8.2）由脑神经、脊神经和部分自主神经系统组成。脊神经共有31对，其中颈神经8对（C1—C8）、胸神经12对（T1—T12）、腰神经5对（L1—L5）、骶神经5对（S1—S5）和尾神经1对（Coc1）。每根脊神经都通过椎间孔伸出椎管。腰神经根和骶神经根在脊髓圆锥处构成称为马尾的神经根。

AUTONOMIC NERVOUS SYSTEM 

自主神经系统

12 The autonomic nervous system is the part of the peripheral nervous system that regulates the basic visceral processes needed for the maintenance of normal bodily functions. It is defined as a motor system that innervates three major types of tissue: cardiac muscle, smooth muscle, and glands. The autonomic system consists of two major divisions: the sympathetic nervous system and the parasympathetic nervous system. These often function in antagonistic ways. 

自主神经是周围神经系统的一部分，控制着维持正常机体功能所需要的基本内脏活动。自主神经也被界定为运动系统，因为它为3种主要组织提供神经支配：心肌、平滑肌和腺体。自主神经系统包括两个主要部分：交感神经系统和副交感神经系统。这两个神经系统的功能经常相互拮抗。

DISEASES OF NEUROPSYCHIATRIC ORIGIN

神经精神性疾病

13 This critical signaling between neurons is mediated by chemical transmitter molecules known as neurotransmitters. Neurotransmitters are synthesized by nerve cells and released from one cell to another across the synapse. In some types of nerves the synapses do not possess receptors, in which case interneuronal communication is achieved by electrical transmission. In many neuropsychiatric diseases alterations in the levels of transmitter substances appear to play a major role in pathogenesis. 

神经细胞之间关键的信号传递是通过一种叫神经递质的化学递质分子来进行的。神经递质由神经细胞合成，通过突触由一个神经细胞释放到另一个神经细胞。在有些类别的神经中，突触没有受体。在这种情况下，神经细胞间的传导通过电传递完成。在很多神经精神性疾病中，递质水平的改变在致病中起到主要作用。

Psychiatric Diseases

精神疾病

14 Mental illnesses affect various facets of personality, purposeful behavior, abstract thinking, creativity, emotion, and mood. These diseases include obsessive-compulsive personality disorder⑭, dementia, schizophrenia, major depression, and manic disorders. 

精神疾病影响性格、有目的行为、抽象思维、创造力、情感及情绪等各个方面。精神疾病包括强迫型人格障碍、痴呆、精神分裂症、重度抑郁症和躁狂症。

15 Schizophrenia in its severe form is a catastrophic mental illness that begins in adolescence or early adult life. It is believed that heredity plays an important role in the genesis of the disease. However, other studies suggest that nongenetic factors like an excess of the neurotransmitter substance dopamine are also influential. 

重型精神分裂症是一种灾难性精神疾病，该病始于青少年期或成年早期。有人认为遗传因素在该病的起源中起很重要的作用，但是其他研究显示，诸如神经递质多巴胺过多等非遗传因素也有影响。

16 Pathological disturbances of mood, ranging from severe depression to manic behavior, are common forms of mental illnesses. Severe depression is characterized by despondency, diminished interest in most or all activities, weight fluctuation not due to dieting, disruption in sleep patterns, psychomotor agitation or retardation, feelings of worthlessness, excessive quiet, and recurrent thoughts of death or suicide. Manic behavior involves a period in which an expansive, elevated, or irritable mood persists abnormally. During this episode symptoms such as increased talkativeness, distractibility, decreased need for sleep, inflated self-esteem, and excessive involvement in pleasurable yet risky activities may be present. Major depression is associated with decreased brain levels of the neurotransmitters norepinephrine and serotonin, and the most effective therapy consists of drugs that inhibit the breakdown of these compounds. 

从重度抑郁症到躁狂行为等一系列病理性情绪紊乱都是心理疾病的常见表现。重度抑郁症的特征有意志消沉、对大多数或者所有活动的兴趣下降、没有节食但出现体重波动、睡眠习惯破坏、精神运动性激动或迟滞、无用感、极度安静、反复出现死亡或自杀的念头。躁狂行为指一段时期内自高自大、情绪高涨和易激惹的情绪异常持续存在。发作期可能出现诸如话多、注意力涣散、睡眠需求减少、自尊心膨胀、对于愉悦而危险的活动过分投入等症状。重症抑郁症与大脑神经递质去甲肾上腺素和血清素的浓度下降有关，因此最有效的治疗包括使用能够抑制这些复合物分解的药物。

Neurological Diseases 

神经性疾病

17 Alzheimer disease⑮ and Parkinson disease⑯ are two age-related neurological diseases, and to varying degrees they manifest as deterioration of mental function that involves the loss of memory and of acquired intellectual skills. This deterioration is referred to as dementia. 

阿尔兹海默病和帕金森病是两种与年龄相关的神经性疾病，它们表现出不同程度的心智退化，包括丧失记忆力和获得性智力技能。这种退化称为痴呆。

Cardiovascular System 

心血管系统

1 Cardiovascular system1 conveys blood through vessels to and from all parts of the body, carrying nutrients and oxygen to tissues and removing carbon dioxide2 and other wastes. It is a closed tubular system in which the blood is propelled by the heart. Two circuits, the pulmonary and the systemic, consist of arterial, capillary, and venous components.

心血管系统通过血管将血液输送到全身各个部位，为组织提供营养物质和氧气，并将全身各处的血液运回心脏，将二氧化碳和其他废物排出体外。心血管系统是一套封闭的管道系统，其中心脏推动血液流动。血液循环有两个循环体系，即肺循环和体循环，由动脉、毛细血管和静脉组成。

ANATOMY 

解剖结构

Heart 

心脏

2 The human adult heart is normally slightly larger than a clenched fist. The wall of the heart consists of three distinct layers — the epicardium, the myocardium, and the endocardium.

成年人的心脏通常比自身紧握的拳头稍大。心壁由明显的3层构成，即心外膜、心肌层和心内膜。

3 The heart (Fig.11.1 & 11.2) is divided by septa into four chambers: two atria and two ventricles. The right atrium receives blood from all tissues except the lungs via the superior and inferior venae cavae3 and the coronary sinus4. Blood flows from the right atrium to the right ventricle. The pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle to the lungs.

心脏（图11.1和图11.2）被间隔分为4个腔室：两个心房和两个心室。右心房通过上腔静脉、下腔静脉和冠状窦接收来自除肺之外的其他所有组织的血液。血液从右心房流入右心室，肺动脉将缺氧血从右心室输送到肺。
 

 

4 The left atrium receives the four pulmonary veins, which bring oxygenated blood5 from the lungs. Blood flows from the left atrium into the left ventricle. Blood is forced from this chamber through the aorta to all parts of the body except the lungs.

左心房与4条肺静脉相连，接收来自肺的含氧血。血液从左心房流入左心室，血液从左心室泵入主动脉，并输送到除肺之外的其他身体部位。

5 The heart is equipped with valves to prevent backflow of blood. There are two types of valves located in the heart: the atrioventricular valves6 (tricuspid and mitral) and the semilunar valves7 (pulmonary and aortic).

心脏有瓣膜，可以阻止血液倒流。心脏瓣膜分为两种：房室瓣（三尖瓣和二尖瓣）和半月瓣（肺动脉瓣和主动脉瓣）。

6 The heart possesses a vascular system of its own, called the coronary arterial system8. In the most common distribution, this comprises two major coronary arteries, the right and the left. Venous blood from the heart is carried through cardiac veins. 

心脏有自己的血管系统，称为冠状动脉系统。最常见的分布情况下，它包括左右两条大冠状动脉。来自心脏的静脉血通过心静脉运送。

7 Regular beating of the heart is achieved as a result of the inherent rhythmicity of cardiac muscle, referred to as automaticity or autorhythmicity. This is maintained by an elaborate conducting (conduction) system9 (Fig.11.3) that consists of two nodes: the sinoatrial node (SA node) and atrioventricular node (AV node), the bundle of HIS (atrioventricular bundle)10, and Purkinje fibers11.

心脏有节律地跳动，是心肌本身能节律性地运动的结果，称为自动节律性，是通过精巧的传导系统（图11.3）所维持，传导系统包括窦房结、房室结、希氏束（房室束）和浦肯野纤维。
 

 

8 A heartbeat is a two-part pumping action that takes about a second. As blood collects the atria, the electrical signal sent out by the SA node causes the atria to contract. The blood in the atria is pushed through the tricuspid and mitral valves into the ventricles. This pumping phase is called the diastole. The second part of the pumping phase begins when the ventricles are full of blood. The electrical signals from the SA node travel to the ventricles, causing them to contract. This is called systole. 

一次心跳由两个泵血动作完成，大约持续1秒钟。血液进入心房，由窦房结发出的电信号引起心房收缩，挤压血液从心房经过三尖瓣和二尖瓣进入心室，这个泵血期称为舒张期。当心室充满血液时，第2个泵血期开始。窦房结产生的电信号传至心室，引起心室收缩，称为收缩期。

Blood Vessels 

血管

9 There are three types of vessels — arteries(Fig.11.4), veins(Fig.11.5), and capillaries. The arteries carry blood away from the heart. The capillaries connect the arteries to veins by venules. Finally, the veins carry the blood back to the heart. Arteries and veins have three layers of tissue: the tunica intima, the tunica media, and the tunica adventitia. 

人体内有3种血管：动脉（图11.4）、静脉（图11.5）和毛细血管。动脉把血液带离心脏，毛细血管通过微静脉连接动脉和静脉，最后静脉血管把血液带回心脏。动脉和静脉有3层组织：内膜、中膜和外膜。

Blood

血液

10 Blood consists of 55% plasma and 45% formed elements which include erythrocytes (red cells), leukocytes (white cells) and platelets (thrombocytes). Plasma is the clear, straw colored, liquid portion of blood. Erythrocytes are formed by a process called erythropoiesis. They contain hemoglobin, which is capable of combining with oxygen and carbon dioxide. Leukocytes fall into two large categories: granulocytes and agranulocytes. The former, originating in bone marrow, are further classified as neutrophils, eosinophils and basophils. The latter, originating in lymphatic organs, include lymphocytes and monocytes. The primary function of white blood cells is to engulf and destroy foreign substances, a process called phagocytosis. Platelets originate in red bone marrow from megakaryocytes. Platelets function in clotting mechanism. 

血液由55％的血浆和45％的有形成分构成。有形成分包括红细胞、白细胞和血小板。血浆是血液中透明、浅黄色液体部分。红细胞通过红细胞生成过程产生，含血红蛋白，能与氧和二氧化碳结合。白细胞分为两大类：粒细胞和无粒白细胞。粒细胞源于骨髓，可细分为中性粒细胞、嗜酸性粒细胞和嗜碱性粒细胞。无粒白细胞源于淋巴器官，包括淋巴细胞和单核细胞。白细胞的主要功能是吞噬并破坏异物，这个过程称为吞噬作用。血小板源于红骨髓中的巨核细胞，其功能在于凝血机制。
 

 

CARDIOVASCULAR DISEASES 

心血管疾病

Congenital Heart Diseases 

先天性心脏病

11 Congenital heart disease is one of the important types of diseases affecting the cardiovascular system. In most patients the causes appear to fit in the middle of a continuum from primarily genetic to primarily environmental. 

先天性心脏病是对心血管系统有重要影响的一类疾病。先天性心脏病的病因有的主要是基因因素，有的主要是环境因素，但对大多数病人来说，这两种因素各占一半。

12 The presence of a septal defect12, atrial or ventricular, allows blood to be shunted from the left side of the heart to the right, with an increase in blood flow and volume within the pulmonary circulation. Ventricular septal defects are often combined with other congenital cardiac defects. The best-known of these is tetralogy of Fallot13, named for the French physician Étienne-Louis- Arthur Fallot. In this condition there is a ventricular septal defect, pulmonary stenosis, deviation of the aorta to override the ventricular septum above the ventricular septal defect, and right ventricular hypertrophy. 

房间隔或室间隔缺损使血液从左心分流至右心，导致肺循环血液速度和流量升高。室间隔缺损通常伴随其他先天性心脏缺陷，其中最著名的例子是以法国医生艾蒂安·路易斯·亚瑟·法洛命名的“法洛四联征。”该病包括室间隔缺损、肺动脉狭窄、主动脉偏移骑跨于缺损的室间隔上方，以及右心室肥大。

Acquired Heart Diseases 

后天性心脏病

13 Acquired heart diseases include coronary artery disease14, coronary heart disease15, rheumatic heart disease16, diseases of the pulmonary vessels and the aorta, diseases of the tissues of the heart, and diseases of the heart valves. 

后天性心脏病包括冠状动脉疾病、冠心病、风湿性心脏病、肺血管疾病和主动脉疾病、心脏组织疾病和心脏瓣膜疾病。

14 Coronary artery disease is commonly used as a synonym for the more specific condition of atheromatous intrusion into the artery lumen (cavity). Coronary heart disease is a term used to describe the symptoms and features that can result from advanced coronary artery disease. The same symptoms are also diagnosed as ischemic heart disease17 because the symptoms result from the development of myocardial ischemia. There is no one-to-one relationship between coronary atherosclerosis18 and the clinical symptoms of coronary artery disease or between coronary artery disease and coronary heart disease. Although coronary artery disease is most frequently caused by atherosclerosis, inflammation of the blood vessels may, in rare cases, cause obstructive lesions of the coronary vessels. 

冠状动脉疾病更具体地讲是冠状动脉腔出现粥样化病变，而冠状动脉疾病一词通常成了它的同义词。冠心病通常用来描述由晚期冠状动脉疾病所致的症状和特征。同样的症状也可以诊断为缺血性心脏病，因为这些症状都是由心肌缺血发展而来。冠状动脉粥样硬化与冠状动脉疾病的临床症状之间没有一一对应的关系，冠状动脉疾病与冠心病之间也没有一一对应的关系。虽然冠状动脉疾病大多数情况下是由动脉粥样硬化引起的，但是有些罕见病例中，血管炎症也可能引起冠状血管的阻塞病变。

15 The term angina pectoris19 was first used in 1772 by the British physician William Heberden. Heberden’s initial description is still accurate; however, there are no truly characteristic symptoms of angina pectoris. Whereas the chest discomfort may be variously described as “constricting,” “suffocating,” “crushing,” “heavy,” or “squeezing,” there are many patients in whom the quality of the sensation is imprecise. The discomfort is usually, but not always, behind the breastbone, but pain radiating to the throat or jaw or down the inner sides of either arm is common. 

“心绞痛”这一术语首次于1772年由英国医生威廉·赫伯登使用。虽然他对心绞痛最初的描述依然准确，但是心绞痛并没有真正的典型症状。尽管病人对胸部不适的描述可能各种各样，如“紧缩感”、“窒息感”、“压榨感”、“沉重感”或“挤压感”，但是很多病人对这种感觉的特性并不确切。不适通常但不总是位于胸骨后面，而疼痛向喉部、下巴或者由上肢内侧向下辐射比较常见。

16 When coronary arteriography reveals relatively isolated, incompletely obstructive lesions, there are two alternative treatments — medication or coronary angioplasty. When coronary arteriography reveals a severe blockage of the left main coronary artery or proximally in one or more of the major arteries, coronary artery bypass graft (CABG)20 surgery may be necessary. 

当冠状动脉造影显示有相对孤立且不完整的阻塞性病灶时，有两种可供选择的治疗，即药物治疗或冠状动脉修复术。如果冠状动脉造影显示左主冠状动脉或近侧一条或多条动脉出现严重阻塞，可能需要进行冠状动脉旁路移植术。

17 Myocardial infarction is characterized by cellular death (necrosis) of a segment of the heart muscle. The outstanding clinical feature of myocardial infarction is pain, similar in many respects to that of angina pectoris. The important difference is that the pain lasts for a much longer period — at least half an hour and usually for several hours and perhaps for days. The pain is described as “crushing,” “compressing,” and “like a vise” and is often associated with some difficulty in breathing. 

心肌梗死的特征是部分心肌细胞死亡（坏死），其显著临床特点为疼痛，在许多方面与心绞痛类似。两者最主要的区别在于心肌梗死的疼痛持续时间要长很多，至少半小时，通常长达数小时，也可数天。病人把疼痛描述为“压榨感”、“压迫感”、“像被钳住似的”，而且常常伴有呼吸困难。

18 Rheumatic heart disease results from inflammation of the endocardium, myocardium, and pericardium that occurs during acute rheumatic fever, an infection with pyogenic Streptococcus. 

风湿性心脏病由心内膜、心肌和心包炎症引起，发生于化脓性链球菌感染引起的急性风湿热。

19 Other cardiovascular diseases include pulmonary heart disease (cor pulmonale21), hypertension, arteriosclerosis, aneurysms, endocarditis, pericarditis, arrhythmia (tachycardia, flutter, bradycardia, fibrillation, and heart block), congestive heart failure (CHF)22, thrombophlebitis, phlebothrombosis, varicose veins, syncope, and physiological shock. 

其他心血管疾病包括肺源性心脏病（肺心病）、高血压、动脉粥样硬化、动脉瘤、心内膜炎、心包炎、心律不齐（心动过速、扑动、心动过缓、纤维性震颤和传导阻滞）、充血性心力衰竭、血栓性静脉炎、静脉血栓形成、静脉曲张、昏厥和生理性休克。

EVALUATING THE CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM 

心血管系统评估

20 Certain diagnostic techniques with respect to the heart and blood vessels are important factors in determining the degree of disease and their appropriate medical and surgical treatment. 

在确定疾病程度及其合适的医学和手术治疗时，特定的心血管诊断技术至关重要。

21 Angiocardiography permits direct visualization of the chambers and great vessels of the heart from injections of dyes that are opaque to X-rays. Anatomic defects, such as congenital and acquired lesions, can be detected readily. 

心血管造影术通过将不透X线的造影剂注入心腔和大血管，从而直接获得心腔和大血管的影像。这种技术可以很容易检查出先天性病灶和获得性病灶等解剖结构的缺陷。

22 Arteriography of the coronary arteries permits identification, localization, and assessment of the extent of obstructive lesions within these arteries. It is the most important means of defining the presence and severity of coronary atherosclerosis and, in conjunction with left ventriculography, the related state of myocardial function. 

冠状动脉造影术能够识别、定位及评估动脉内的阻塞性病灶。它是确定冠状动脉粥样硬化的有无、明确其严重程度的最重要手段，如果与左心室造影术结合使用，也是界定心肌功能相关状态的最重要手段。

23 The term echocardiography refers to a group of tests that use ultrasound to examine the heart and record information in the form of echoes, or reflected sonic waves. Radionuclide imaging (radioactive nuclides) provides a safe, quantitative evaluation of cardiac function and a direct measurement of myocardial blood flow and myocardial metabolism. 

超声心动描记术指利用超声波检查心脏的一组检测，并将检测信息以回波或反射声波的方式记录下来。放射性核素成像能安全地对心脏功能作出定量评价，并且能够直接测量出心肌的血流情况和新陈代谢情况。

Respiratory System①

呼吸系统

ANATOMY OF THE RESPIRATORY SYSTEM 

呼吸系统的解剖结构

1 The airways (Fig.12.1) can be divided into upper and lower airway systems. The upper airway system comprises the nose and the paranasal cavities, called sinuses, the pharynx, or throat, and partly also the oral cavity. The lower airway system consists of the larynx, the trachea, the stem bronchi, and all the airways ramifying intensively within the lungs, such as the intrapulmonary bronchi, the bronchioles, and the alveolar ducts②. For respiration, the collaboration of other organ systems is clearly essential. The diaphragm and the intercostal muscles③ of the chest wall play an essential role by generating, under the control of the central nervous system, the pumping action on the lung.

呼吸道（图12.1）可分为上呼吸道系统和下呼吸道系统。上呼吸道系统包括鼻、鼻窦（鼻腔旁的腔室）、咽和口腔的一部分，下呼吸道系统包括喉、气管、支气管干及肺内大量分支形成的各级气道，如肺内支气管、细支气管和肺泡管等。显然，呼吸与其他器官系统的协同作用十分重要。在中枢神经系统的控制下，膈肌和胸壁肋间肌的运动使肺产生吸气和呼气动作。
 

 

Nose 

鼻

2 The nose is the external protuberance of the nasal cavity. It is subdivided into a left and right canal by the nasal septum. Each canal opens to the face by a nostril and into the pharynx by the choana. The floor of the nasal cavity is formed by the palate. 

鼻是鼻腔向外突起的部分，被鼻中隔分为左右两个鼻腔。鼻腔经鼻孔开口于脸部，经鼻后孔与咽部相通。鼻腔的底部由腭构成。

3 On each side, the intranasal space communicates with the paranasal sinuses and also, via the nasolacrimal duct, with the lacrimal apparatus④ in the corner of the eye. 

每一侧的鼻腔与鼻旁窦相通，也通过鼻泪管与位于眼角的泪器相通。

Pharynx 

咽

4 The pharynx can be divided into the nasopharynx, the oral pharynx or oropharynx, and the hypopharynx. Air from the nasal cavity flows into the larynx, and food from the oral cavity is routed to the esophagus directly behind the larynx. The epiglottis functions as a lid to the larynx and, during the act of swallowing, controls the traffic of air and food. 

咽分为鼻咽、口咽和下咽3部分。空气从鼻腔进入喉，而食物从口腔直接进入位于喉后面的食管。会厌的功能像喉的盖子，吞咽时控制空气和食物的流动路线。

Larynx 

喉

5 The larynx (Fig. 12.2) serves a dual function: as an air canal to the lungs and a controller of its access, and as the organ of phonation. Sound is produced by forcing air through a glottis formed by the vocal cords⑤. The laryngeal skeleton consists of nine cartilages: three separate (the thyroid, the epiglottis, and the cricoid), six paired (the arytenoid, corniculate and cuneiform). 

喉（图12.2）具有双重功能：一是空气进入肺的通道，同时控制空气的进出；二是发声器官，挤压的空气经过声带构成的声门而产生声音。喉的骨架由9块软骨构成：3个单一软骨（甲状软骨、会厌软骨和环状软骨）和3对成对软骨（杓状软骨、小角软骨和楔状软骨）。

Trachea and Stem Bronchi

气管和支气管干

6 Below the larynx lies the trachea. Its wall is stiffened by 16 to 20 characteristic horseshoe-shaped, incomplete cartilage rings that open toward the back and are embedded in a dense connective tissue. At its lower end, the trachea divides in an inverted Y into the two stem (or main) bronchi, one each for the left and right lung. 

气管位于喉的下部，气管壁由16～20个“C”形软骨支撑，软骨环缺口向后，被致密结缔组织所包裹。在气管下端，气管分叉，形成倒“Y”形，成为左右两个支气管干，左右肺各一支。
 

 

Structure of the Airway Tree

气道树的结构

7 Functionally the intrapulmonary airway system can be subdivided into three zones, a proximal, purely conducting zone, a peripheral, purely gas-exchanging zone, and a transitional zone in between, where both functions grade into one another.

从功能上讲，肺内气道系统可分为3个区：纯粹导气的近端区、纯粹进行气体交换的周围区和位于两者之间且两种功能相互过渡的过渡区。

8 The conducting airways comprise the trachea, the two stem bronchi, the bronchi, and the bronchioles. The last purely conductive airway generations in the lung are the terminal bronchioles. Distally, the airway structure is greatly altered by the appearance of cuplike outpouchings from the walls. These form minute air chambers and represent the first gas-exchanging alveoli on the airway path. 

传导气道包括气管、左右两个支气管干、支气管和细支气管。肺内纯粹导气的最后一级气道为终末细支气管。在远端，气道管壁向外膨胀，出现杯状的囊，使气道结构发生极大变化。这些小囊形成微小的气泡腔，是整个气道上能最早进行气体交换的肺泡。

9 The gas-exchange region begins with the alveoli of the first generation of respiratory bronchioles. Distally, the frequency of alveolar outpocketings increases rapidly, until the whole wall is formed by alveoli. The airways are then called alveolar ducts and alveolar sacs⑥. 

气体交换区始于一级呼吸性细支气管的肺泡。在远端，气道壁向外膨胀形成肺泡的频率快速增加，直到整个气道壁都由肺泡构成，此时气道分别称为肺泡管和肺泡囊。

Lungs 

肺

10 The organ lung is parted into the left lung and the right lung. The space between them is filled by the mediastinum, which corresponds to a connective tissue space containing the heart, major blood vessels, the trachea with the stem bronchi, the esophagus, and the thymus gland. The inside of the thoracic cavities and the lung surface are covered with serous membranes, respectively the parietal pleura⑦ and the visceral pleura⑧. 

肺分为左肺和右肺，两肺之间是纵隔，对应由结缔组织构成的区域，包括心脏、大血管、气管和与之相连的支气管干、食管和胸腺等。胸腔内壁和肺表面被浆膜覆盖，分别称作胸膜壁层和胸膜脏层。

11 Although the hilum is the only place where the lungs are secured to surrounding structures, the lungs are maintained in close apposition to the thoracic wall by a negative pressure between visceral and parietal pleurae. A thin film of extracellular fluid⑨ between the pleurae enables the lungs to move smoothly along the walls of the cavity during breathing.

虽然肺门是肺与周围结构相连的唯一地方，但是肺能够与胸壁保持紧密相连，靠的是胸膜壁层和胸膜脏层之间的负压。胸膜间有一层细胞外液形成的薄膜，使肺在呼吸时能沿着胸腔壁自由活动。

12 Air moves in and out of the lungs in response to differences in pressure. When the air pressure within the alveolar spaces falls below atmospheric pressure⑩, air enters the lungs (inspiration); when the air pressure within the alveoli exceeds atmospheric pressure, air is blown from the lungs (expiration). 

因为压力差，空气可以进入肺或从肺排出。当肺泡内的气压小于大气压时，空气进入肺，即为吸气；当肺泡内的气压超过大气压时，空气从肺呼出，即为呼气。

SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS

体征和症状

13 Cough is a particularly important sign of all diseases that affect any part of the bronchial tree⑪. A productive cough⑫, cough producing sputum, is the most important manifestation of inflammatory or malignant diseases of the major airways, of which bronchitis is a common example. Hemoptysis, the presence of blood in the sputum, may indicate the presence of inflammation, capillary damage, or a tumor. Hemoptysis is also a classic sign of tuberculosis of the lungs.

在影响支气管树任何一个部位的所有疾病中，咳嗽是特别重要的体征。排痰性咳嗽是大气道炎症或恶性病变最重要的表现，支气管炎是个常见例子。咯血，即痰中带血，可能表明有炎症、毛细血管损伤或肿瘤，同时也是肺结核的典型症状。

14 The second most important symptom of lung disease is dyspnea, or shortness of breath. The shortness of breath may vary in severity, but in diseases such as emphysema, it is constantly present. Severe fibrosis of the lung may also cause severe and unremitting dyspnea. 

肺病的第2个最重要的症状是呼吸困难或气短。气短严重程度不一，但是有些疾病如肺气肿总是有此症状。严重肺纤维化也会导致严重的连续性呼吸困难。

15 Chest pain may be an early symptom of lung disease, but it is most often associated with an attack of pneumonia, in which case it is due to an inflammation of the pleura that follows the onset of the pneumonic process. 

胸痛可能是肺病的早期症状，但胸痛又常常因肺炎发作而出现。肺炎引起的胸痛是由肺炎发展过程中引起的胸膜发炎所造成的。

MAJOR DISEASES 

重大疾病

Pneumonia 

肺炎

16 Before effective antibiotics became available, pneumonia was the respiratory disease responsible for the greatest mortality and consequently was one of the most-feared diseases. The most common form of the disease, Streptococcal pneumonia⑬ (sometimes called pneumococcal pneumonia), is caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae⑭. This form of pneumonia begins abruptly with a high fever and severe malaise followed by natural resolution in survivors after several days or longer. In some cases, infection is followed by complications, such as a lung abscess, pleurisy, or heart failure. 

在有效的抗生素问世之前，肺炎在呼吸系统疾病中死亡率最高，因此成为令人最恐惧的疾病之一。肺炎最常见的形式为链球菌肺炎（有时称为肺炎球菌性肺炎），由肺炎链球菌引起。这种肺炎起病急，伴高热和严重不适。在幸存者中，这些症状在数天或更长时间之后自行消退。在有些病例中，感染后会发生并发症，如肺脓肿、胸膜炎和心力衰竭等。

Tuberculosis

肺结核

17 Of all the lung diseases caused by bacteria, pulmonary tuberculosis (Fig. 12.3) is historically by far the most important. In its classic form, tuberculosis first causes pulmonary inflammation at the apices of the lungs⑮, and it may progress slowly to form a chronic cavity in this region. 

从历史的角度来说，到目前为止，在所有由细菌引起的肺病中，肺结核最为重要。肺结核的典型表现是，首先结核引起肺尖炎症，然后病情缓慢发展，在此区域形成慢性的空腔。
 

 

18 Streptomycin was the first clinically successful antituberculous drug; however, it is only occasionally used today. Most cases of tuberculosis infection are treated with isoniazid. In the case of active tuberculosis disease, the combination of isoniazid, rifampin, pyrazinamide, and ethambutol is often used for a period of six months to a year. 

链霉素是第一个在临床上取得成功的抗结核药物，但是目前只是偶尔使用。大多数肺结核病人都使用异烟肼进行治疗。对于活动性肺结核，需联合服用异烟肼、利福平、吡嗪酰胺和乙胺丁醇进行半年至一年的治疗。

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease 

慢性阻塞性肺病

19 Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)⑯ refers broadly to a group of conditions that cause irreversible respiratory impairment by increasing obstruction to airflow through the bronchi of the lungs. COPD typically has two components which may be present to varying degrees: chronic obstructive bronchitis and pulmonary emphysema. 

慢性阻塞性肺病（简称慢阻肺）广义上指一组疾病，这些疾病由于增加了空气通过肺内支气管的阻力，从而导致不可逆转的呼吸受损。慢阻肺通常包括两种疾患，只是严重程度可能不同，即慢性阻塞性支气管炎和肺气肿。

Silicosis and Black Lung Disease 

矽肺病和黑肺病

20 Silicosis is a hazard in any occupation in which workers are exposed to silica dust, particularly rock drilling above or below ground, quarrying, or grinding with a wheel containing silica. Coal dust alone, even if its silica content is very low, causes a distinctive pattern of change in the lung known as coalworker’s pneumoconiosis (also called black lung). 

矽肺病是工人暴露于硅尘环境中而引起的职业病，特别是地面或地下进行岩石开凿、采石或者使用含二氧化硅的砂轮进行研磨。仅仅是煤尘，即使含硅量非常低，也会引起肺部显著的形态变化，称为煤工尘肺，也称黑肺。

21 Other diseases of the respiratory system include common cold⑰, influenza, whooping cough (pertussis)⑱ , asthma, bronchitis, bronchiectasis, pleurisy, pulmonary embolism⑲ , pulmonary edema⑳ , and lung cancer. 

其他呼吸系统疾病包括普通感冒、流感、百日咳、哮喘、支气管炎、支气管扩张、胸膜炎、肺栓塞、肺水肿和肺癌。
